Case Studies
Case Study 1: Implementation of ACO (acountable care organization) analytics platform, with $3-5 million opportunities identified.

Akron Children's Collaborative is an ACO with 100,000 children's lives covered via CareSource/OH Dept of Health with PMPM (per member per month) model, $2 million annual loss out of $120 million net patient annual revenue.

I led a team of 30 professionals, built an Enterprise Data Warehouse (EDW) to integrate clinical, claims, payment, risk tiers, social determinants of health (SDoH) to identify cost saving opportunities while improving quality outcomes on population health.

I led the team to partner with Health Catalyst on implementation of Data Operating System, integrated 4 sources of data input, configured analytics applications, and identified $3-5 million annual cost saving opportunities on care management processes (prioritization by diagnostic codes, referral, clinician behavior and patient journey changes).

Case Study 2: Successful internal AI startup at a multi-generation family owned enterprise with 10x project ROI (return on investment).

As a family-owned distributor with $5+ billion revenue and $150 million EBITDA, US Venture has under-invested in data and analytics capabilities, taking longer time to make decisions with less accuracy and lower confidence.

I started an internal “data and analytics” startup with commitment from CEO and Board of Directors, support and accountability from division presidents, and alliance with CFO and Operations teams to measure ROI and improve processes.

I led the team and built a portfolio of major projects, achieved 10-times ROI and visibility of entire program breakeven in 5 years; the team grew from 3 database developers to 18 members in 3 years to cover the full spectrum of data and analytics capabilities, elevating US Venture as the industry leader in transportation consumable distribution.

Case Study 3: New product revenue stream generation at a PE (private equity) portfolion company with 100% revenue growth in 5 years.

Traditional credit lending and marketing decisions were made by transaction patterns and demographics, missing an important aspect of direct-measured consumer households’ wealth.

I led a team of 10 highly skilled professionals (data scientist, engineers, statisticians, econometricians), developed 3 new products that better profile US consumer households direct-measured financial liquid assets (geographic distribution, size, asset mix, and trends), to be combined with traditional data sources, so we could improve accuracy of targeting marketing and risk control.

We developed 3 major products and drove annual revenue from $20 million to $40 million in 5 years despite the 2008 Great Recession.

IXI was founded in 1994, owned by Private Equity firm Core Capital Partners, and acquired in 2011 by Equifax for $124 million.

Case Study 4: Rapid growth to gain market share at a PE portfolio company in financial service.

U.S. educational loans saw a big drop in interest rates, fueling huge volume of student loan consolidation. How to best target borrowers with a limited marketing budget?

I developed a suite of 3 predictive models to target the best prospects for loan consolidation, with higher response rate, higher average loan amount, and lower delinquency rate.

Our annual marketing budget was $50 million. I contributed to the higher efficiency of marketing budget allocation, saving $5 million expenses, and reduced future delinquency loss amount.

Collegiate Funding Services Inc. was founded in 2002, owned by Private Equity firm Lightyear Capital; it went public as CFSI on NASDAQ in 2004 and was acquired in 2005 by JPMorgan Chase for $663 million.
